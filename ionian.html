Ionian

Yep!
Ionian is just a fancy word to say the major scale. The major scale, first popularized by Julie Andrews in the 1965 classic, The Sound of Music 1, serves as the foundation for just about everything discussed on this website. It inspired our logo.

To build the major scale we divide up an octave (that is two notes, one with double the frequency/pitch of the other) into 12 equal distances or intervals. We call these half-steps. The distance between any two adjacent keys on the piano is a half-step.
